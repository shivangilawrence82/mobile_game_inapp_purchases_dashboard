# mobile_game_inapp_purchases_dashboard
This project analyzes player behavior and spending patterns in a mobile gaming platform using Power BI. It includes two dashboards: Spending Analysis and Behavioral Analysis. The goal is to understand how demographics, playstyles, and geography influence player behavior and in-app spending trends.

Note:Dataset used for this project was imported from Kaggle.

Dataset:-https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pratyushpuri/mobile-game-in-app-purchases-dataset-2025

Key points analyzed using the dashboards created:-

1)Spending Analysis

i.Revenue by Continent:- Asia stands out as the top revenue generting continent with a significantly higher value compared to others.

ii.Revenue by Game Genre:- The Racing genre leads in total revenue followed by Battle Royale, Strategy and MOBA (Mobile Online Battle Arena) genres.

iii.Average In-app Purchase by Spending Segment:- Whales spend substantially more on average showing the strongest revenue impact.

iv.Revenue by Gender:- Male players contribute the most revenue overall more than double than that of Female players.

v.Correlation between session length and purchase:- A Correlation value of 0.95 indicates a very strong positive relationship sugessting longer gameplay sessions are strongly associated with higher spending.

2)Behavioral Analysis

i.Active Players by Continent:- Asia has the highest number of active players followed by Europe and North America.

ii.Average Session Duration by Continent:- Africa and Australia have the longest average session durations(around 30-32 minutes)

iii.Top Games by Active Players:- Battle Royale and Racing dominate in player count aligning with their strong revenue performance.

iv.Retention Rate by Continent:- Asia again shows the highest retention rate ins=dicating strong user loyalty and repeat engagement.

v.Age Group with Maximum Purchase:- According to the visualiztion, Young Adults tend to spend more on in-app purchases.

vi.Average Purchases per Player:- Players in Asia and Europe make more frequent purchase while North A,erica and Australia show lower purchase frequencies relative to session time.This may suggest regonal difference in spending behavior cultural or economic factors may influence willingness to spend.
